## Guideline 2.1(b) — Information Needed: Business Model

WebNegosyo is a software-as-a-service platform that gives restaurants and food businesses their own branded online ordering website where their customers order food (physical goods). **WebNegosyo Admin** (this app) is the free companion app that merchants use to manage those incoming orders. To be clear up front: **this app is free, contains no purchases of any kind, no in-app purchases, and unlocks no paid digital content.** It is available to the general public — any business can sign up directly inside the app, and anyone can explore it via Demo Mode without an account.

Answers to your questions:

1. **Who are the users that will use the paid content, subscriptions, features, and services in the app?**
   There is no paid content, subscription, or paid feature inside the app. The app's users are restaurant and food-business owners and their staff, who use it for free to view and manage orders from their store. The app is open to the general public — any business can request an account from the in-app sign-up screen, and anyone can try the app in Demo Mode without an account.

2. **Where can users purchase the content, subscriptions, features, and services that can be accessed in the app?**
   Nowhere — there is nothing to purchase in or for the app. The app is free. Separately, merchants engage WebNegosyo for the online-ordering website service (a business service through which their customers buy physical food); that service is arranged on our website (https://webnegosyo.com), entirely outside this app, and the app does not link to or promote that purchase.

3. **What specific types of previously purchased content, subscriptions, features, and services can a user access in the app?**
   None. The app does not unlock any previously purchased digital content. After a merchant's store is set up, the app simply displays that store's operational data: incoming orders, order statuses, daily sales figures, and menu analytics. All features of the app are available to every signed-in merchant equally, and most can be seen in Demo Mode.

4. **Is your app restricted to users who are part of a single company?**
   No. The app is not restricted to any company, partner, employee, or contractor group. Any food or retail business anywhere can become a merchant via the public, self-service sign-up screen inside the app or via our website. The general public can also open the app in Demo Mode without any account.

5. **What paid content, subscriptions, or features are unlocked within your app that do not use in-app purchase?**
   None. No content, subscription, or feature is unlocked by any payment, inside or outside the app.

6. **What features in the app, if any, are intended for use by the general public?**
   All of them. The sign-up screen is open to the general public so any business owner can request an account, and Demo Mode lets anyone — including non-merchants — explore the full app (dashboard, order queue, analytics) without an account. The app is intended for public distribution to any current or prospective merchant.

7. **Are the enterprise services in your app sold to single users, consumers, or for family use?**
   There are no enterprise services sold in the app. The app itself is free for everyone. Our ordering-website service is offered to individual small businesses (often single-owner restaurants), not to enterprises, and is sold outside the app.

8. **What is the maximum number of users that can participate in your app's live, real-time services?**
   The app has no live multi-user services such as conferencing or streaming. Its only real-time feature is that a merchant's own order list updates automatically when their customers place orders. There is no participant limit — each merchant simply sees their own store's orders.

9. **Can users purchase physical goods or services together with digital content in your app?**
   No. Nothing can be purchased in this app at all. Physical goods (food) are purchased by consumers on the merchant's separate ordering website, not in this app, and no digital content is bundled with those purchases.

10. **Is your app designed for use by a limited or specific group of companies?**
    No. Any company or individual business owner can become a client — there is no invitation, contract, or partner requirement. Sign-up is self-service and public, directly inside the app. The app is designed for open, general-public distribution on the App Store.
